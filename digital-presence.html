Part A

Portfolio Audit — claudiatenhoope.com

Claudia ten Hoope is a freelance data scientist and analyst based in the Amsterdam region. Her portfolio site (claudiatenhoope.com) is a WordPress-based single-page site with service descriptions, a portfolio section listing past clients (Schiphol, NS, PostNL, HelloFresh), and contact information. Below is a brief audit of her SEO posture, content quality, and backlink profile.

On-Page SEO Evaluation

Title Tag "Claudia ten Hoope - Freelance data analyst and data scientist" — clear, includes name + both roles, keyword-rich ✓ Good
Meta Description Not visible in source or page markup — likely missing or auto-generated by WordPress. A missed opportunity to drive click-through from SERPs with a compelling value proposition. ⚠ Missing
Headers Uses H2 for major sections (Services, About me, Portfolio, Contact). Logical hierarchy, though no H3 sub-structure within service descriptions — limits semantic depth for crawlers. ✓ Adequate
URLs Clean and descriptive: /data-analysis/, /dashboard-en/. No parameter strings or dynamic IDs. Bilingual structure (/nl/) is properly handled. ✓ Good
Keyword Usage Natural, not stuffed. Core terms ("data scientist," "prediction models," "machine learning") appear organically in body copy alongside tool names (Python, PySpark, Databricks). ✓ Good
Image Alt Text Logo uses alt="Claudia ten Hoope", but service icons use generic filenames (e.g., "Business-person-chart.png") with no descriptive alt text. Missed accessibility and SEO signal. ⚠ Partial

Content Quality

Informative? Yes. Services are clearly described with specific tools listed (Python, SQL, PySpark, Databricks, Streamlit). The "10+ years / 25 companies" claim establishes credibility. ✓ Yes
Targeted? Targeted at potential freelance clients, not recruiters. The language, rates section, and KvK (Dutch Chamber of Commerce) number all signal a B2B services positioning. ✓ Clients
Differentiated? Somewhat. The experience claim and client logos (Schiphol, HelloFresh) are strong, but the site feels templated and lacks a unique voice. No case studies, no blog, no data storytelling examples — it looks like many other freelancer portfolios. ⚠ Weak
1–2 Ideas to Improve Content

1. Add case studies with outcomes. Rather than listing client names (Schiphol, HelloFresh), write a 2–3 sentence outcome per project: "Built a passenger prediction model that reduced forecasting error by X%, saving Y€ in operational costs." Concrete results convert site visitors into leads far more effectively than logos alone.

2. Add a blog or "Insights" section. Even 3–4 short posts (e.g., "How I built a real-time dashboard in Streamlit") would dramatically improve organic search reach by generating long-tail keyword traffic, and would demonstrate thought leadership that distinguishes her from competing freelancers.

Below is my plan to optimize the site for search engines, answer engines, and paid search.

Planned SEO Improvements

Title & Meta Rewrites

Current title: "Gianni Hrousis | Marketing Analytics Portfolio" — good, but can be stronger.

Rewrite to: "Gianni Hrousis | Business Analytics Portfolio — CLV, Regression & Sentiment Analysis"

Add per-page meta descriptions, e.g. for the K9 CLV page: "End-to-end CLV analysis on 3 years of real pet boarding data — RFM segmentation, BG/NBD modeling, cohort retention, and price elasticity using Python, MySQL, and AWS."

Content Additions

About section expansion: Add a keyword-rich paragraph on the homepage that naturally incorporates target terms (business analytics, predictive modeling, Python, R, SQL, Lehigh University).

Results callouts: Add a "Key Finding" summary to each project page (e.g., "Champions segment = 21% of customers, 65.7% of revenue").

Methodology sections: Brief "What I Did" writeups per project generate long-tail traffic and give AI engines structured content to quote.

Internal Linking

Currently, all project pages link back to the homepage, but do not cross-link to each other. Improvement: add contextual cross-links between related projects — e.g., Netflix CLV should link to K9 CLV ("See how I applied CLV modeling to real operational data →"), and the Buying Decisions page should reference Social Listening ("See the sentiment analysis approach used in Assignment 1 →"). Descriptive anchor text (not "click here") strengthens on-page SEO signals.

Technical SEO

Submit a sitemap.xml to Google Search Console — GitHub Pages does not auto-generate one, and without it, Googlebot may not discover all project sub-pages.

Add OG meta tags to each project page (currently only on index.html) to improve social sharing previews when links are posted on LinkedIn or shared by recruiters.

Ensure all images on future pages include descriptive alt text for accessibility and image search indexing.

AEO — Answer Engine Optimization

Answer Engine Optimization targets AI-powered search surfaces like ChatGPT, Google SGE (Search Generative Experience), Perplexity, and voice assistants. The goal is to structure content so that AI systems can easily extract, quote, and surface my portfolio when answering queries about analytics methods, CLV modeling, or marketing data science.

Planned FAQ Section — Homepage

I plan to add a structured FAQ section using <h2> and <h3> tags with clear, plain-language answers. AI engines prioritize content that directly answers questions in concise, well-structured formats. Example FAQs:

FAQ Content — Planned Additions
QuestionStructured Answer
What is RFM segmentation? RFM segments customers by Recency (how recently they purchased), Frequency (how often), and Monetary value (how much they spend). In the K9 Resorts project, RFM identified a Champions segment representing 21% of customers but 65.7% of total revenue — the single most important finding for retention strategy.
What tools does Gianni Hrousis use? Python (pandas, scikit-learn, lifetimes), R, SQL, MySQL, AWS RDS, Tableau, Power BI, and Bloomberg Terminal — applied across projects in customer analytics, NLP sentiment analysis, logistic regression, and CLV modeling.
What is the BG/NBD model? The BG/NBD (Beta-Geometric / Negative Binomial Distribution) model predicts future customer purchases based on transaction history. It estimates two processes simultaneously: how frequently a customer buys and how likely they are to have permanently churned. I used it in the K9 Resorts CLV project to forecast individual customer value over the next 12 months.
What is VADER sentiment analysis? VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) assigns a compound score between −1 (most negative) and +1 (most positive) to text. I used it in the Social Listening project to score 1,536 Sephora product reviews and found that longer reviews express significantly more extreme sentiment (p < 0.001).

Additional AEO tactics: Add bullet summaries at the top of each project page ("Key methods: RFM, BG/NBD, price elasticity analysis"). Use plain-language definitions for technical terms inline — these are exactly what AI systems pull when answering "what is X" queries. Structure answers with semantic HTML headers rather than visual-only styling.

SEM — Paid Search Strategy (Hypothetical)

While I am not currently running Google Ads, the following outlines what a targeted paid search campaign would look like for my portfolio — designed to intercept recruiters and hiring managers during my job search window.

Google Ads — Example Keywords & Strategy
KeywordSearch IntentWhy Bid On It
"marketing data scientist portfolio" Recruiter / evaluator Recruiters actively looking for candidates with analytics portfolios. Medium competition, high conversion intent — the searcher is already evaluating.
"data analytics projects R Python" Evaluator / peer Hiring managers or senior analysts validating a candidate's technical stack. Long-tail enough to keep CPC low while attracting qualified traffic.
"hire marketing analyst entry level" Direct hiring intent Highest-intent keyword — someone actively looking to fill a role. More expensive CPC ($8–15 estimated), but the conversion value (a job) justifies the spend. Useful as a short-burst campaign during peak hiring (April–June).
"CLV RFM analysis Python project" Peer / professional Long-tail, low CPC. Attracts analytics professionals who can validate and share the work — indirect network effect. Also captures students searching for CLV project examples.
"Lehigh University business analytics" Institutional affiliation Geo-adjacent — captures alumni networks, Lehigh-connected recruiters, and institutional brand searches. Very low competition.

Campaign design: For a personal portfolio, SEM is useful for short-term visibility bursts around job search season. Budget $5–10/day on 3–4 long-tail keywords with high recruiter intent. Broad terms like "data analyst" have CPCs of $5–15+ and would be wasteful at this scale. The campaign goal is clicks-to-portfolio, not brand awareness — so optimize for CTR and portfolio time-on-page, not impressions.

Part C

Email Newsletter — Growth Engine

Newsletter Theme

"The Analytics Edge" — weekly insights from a business analytics practitioner. Positioned at the intersection of marketing analytics and real business operations, written from the perspective of someone who has applied CLV modeling, regression analysis, and sentiment analysis to both academic and real-world contexts (K9 Resorts franchise operations). The newsletter is not purely academic or purely technical — it bridges both worlds.

Newsletter Structure (Each Issue)

1 Insight (Short)

A 2–3 sentence analytical observation with a "so what." Example: "Why RFM beats average spend as a customer segmentation tool — and when it breaks down. RFM captures behavioral patterns (recency, frequency) that average-spend-based segments miss entirely. But RFM assumes transaction-level data, which many SaaS businesses don't have in the same form."

1 Project Highlight

Rotate through the four portfolio projects with a "what I built → what I found → what it means" format. Keep to ~150 words per issue. Example: "This week: Florence Books logistic regression. Art book buyers are 3.2× more likely to subscribe to an online book club. The model's 4.28× lift in the top decile means targeted email outreach could cut acquisition costs by 60% vs. mass campaigns."

1 Tool / Tip

A concrete, actionable technical tip. Example: "How to fit a BG/NBD model in Python with the lifetimes library in under 10 lines of code — and why the penalizer_coef parameter matters more than you think for small datasets."

Frequency & Platform

Weekly cadence, sent Tuesday or Wednesday evenings (highest open rates for professional/educational content). Platform: Mailchimp free tier (supports up to 500 contacts with built-in A/B testing). Each email includes UTM-tagged links to the portfolio and LinkedIn profile for attribution tracking.

A/B Test Design

Variable
Subject line — testing descriptive vs. outcome-driven framing
Version A
"My New Marketing Analytics Project"
Descriptive framing — tells the reader what it is, but doesn't reveal why they should care
Version B
"How 21% of Customers Generated 65% of Revenue — and What We Did About It"
Outcome-driven — leads with a concrete, curiosity-triggering result that invites the click
Hypothesis
Version B wins on open rate because it leads with a specific, surprising data point rather than a generic project description. Curiosity gaps drive opens; descriptions do not.
Primary Metric
Open rate — measured at 48 hours post-send to allow for delayed openers
Secondary Metric
Click-through rate (CTR) to the portfolio project page — confirms whether the subject line attracted the right audience, not just clicks
Test Design
Minimum 100 subscribers split 50/50 for statistical significance. Use Mailchimp's built-in A/B test feature with automated winner selection after 48 hours. The winning subject line is then sent to the remaining list (if applicable).

Why Does the Most-Viewed Page Get the Most Traffic?

Expected Pattern

For GitHub Pages portfolios, the homepage (index.html) almost always leads in page views because it is the default entry point — every shared link (resume, LinkedIn, email signature) directs to it. Project pages receive fewer views because visitors must navigate deeper. If the K9 CLV or Social Listening page shows unusually high traffic, it likely means a specific link was shared on LinkedIn or in a recruiter conversation, driving direct traffic to that sub-page.

Where Are Visitors Coming From?

Expected Pattern

Direct traffic typically dominates for GitHub Pages portfolios because most visitors arrive via a shared URL (resume, LinkedIn profile, email). Organic search is usually low without a sitemap submitted to Google Search Console and without proper meta tags. Social traffic (LinkedIn) may appear if portfolio links were included in LinkedIn posts. Referral traffic from GitHub itself is possible if visitors find the repository and click through to the live site.
